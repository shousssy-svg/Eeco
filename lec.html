Impacts of Human Activities on Ecosystems

Human plays a significant role in shaping ecosystems. Our actions can either help sustain ecological balance or contribute to environmental degradation. Understanding both the positive and negative human impacts helps us become more responsible stewards of our natural world.


Negative Impacts of Human Activities

1. Deforestation - happens when trees are cut down to make space for farms, houses, or roads, or through illegal logging. When large areas of forest are removed, plants and animals lose their homes.

In the Philippines, deforestation is common in upland areas where kaingin or slash-and-burn farming is used. During the rainy season, deforested slopes are more likely to slide, putting nearby homes and communities in danger.


2. Pollution - happens when harmful substances enter the air, water, or soil. In the Philippines, pollution is often seen in busy roads, factories, rivers, and coastal areas.


3. Destruction of Coastal Resources - coastal areas are important because they provide food, livelihood, and protection for many Filipino communities. In the Philipines, many people depend on the sea for fishing and daily living.

Harmful fishing practices, such as dynamite fishing and muro-ami, damage coral reefs and kill fish, including young ones.


4. Overexploitation of Resources - occurs when people catch too many fish, hunt too many animals, or use land excessively. When this happens, plants and animals do not have enough time to recover, making ecosystems weaker.

In the Philippines, resources such as fish, forests, and land are often heavily used to meet human needs.


5. Urbanization and Land Conversion - when forests, grasslands, or open areas are turned into buildings or farmlands, animals lose their natural homes and struggle to find food, water, and safe spaces to live. Over time, this can reduce the number of wildlife in an area.


Positive Impacts of Human Activities

1. Reforestation and Tree Planting - trees help the environment healthy by cleaning air, preventing soil erosion, and providing shelter for animals. Learning about reforestation helps students understand how eople can help restore damaged environments.

Reforestation heppens when trees are planted in areas where forests were cut down. In the Philippines, schools, barangays, and upland areas often organize tree-planting activities.


2. Wildlife Conservation - focuses on protecting animals and their natural habitats. Many species in the Philippines are found nowhere else, which makes their protection especially important. Learning about wildlife conservation helps students understand why some animals need special care.


3. Sustainable Practices - people use natural resources every day for energy, food, and daily needs. Sustainable practices help ensure these resources are not used up too quickly. Learning about sustainability helps students understand how daily choices can affect the environment.


4. Environmental Laws and Education - In the Philippines, environmental laws guide people in caring for natural resources. Learning about these laws helps students understand the importance of shared responsibility.


5. Ecotourism and Community Programs - nature provides beauty, resources, and livelihoods for many communities. Ecotourism allows people to enjoy natural places helping protect them. Learning about ecotourims helps students understand how tourism can benefit both people and the environment.
